Kubernetes

A short introduction

What is kubernetes?

From the docs:

Kubernetes is an open-source system for automating deployment, scaling, and management of containerized applications

Essentially, kubernetes allows us to run applications on VMs from configurations, with a suite of tools that aid uptime, scaling, and management

Features

We make use of a variety of features at INSIGHT, but kubernetes is known for its flexibility:

Manage infrastructure as code with kustomize, helm, and flux. Progressively roll out changes to containers, and maintain ability to roll-back if necessary.
Automatically restart containers that fail, reschedule deployments after failure, and kill containers that don't respond to a health-check.
Connect to external secret repositories or deploy internally without rebuilding images or exposing secrets in-configuration
Mount containers as drives, from local storage, over networks, or cloud providers such as AWS or GCP
Manage scaling with commands, UI, or automatically based on CPU usage, tasks, or other metrics
